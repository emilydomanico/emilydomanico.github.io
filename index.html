About

Click HERE for a printable CV.

I am a cartographer interested in how map design informs map use—particularly in the context of infrastructural landscapes. I recently completed a Master’s of Science in Geography from Penn State, and I hold a BA in History of Art with a minor in Chemistry from UC Berkeley. My thesis research focused on map use in logistical spaces, specifically public transit control centers. From the perspective of user-centered design, I studied the role of maps and schematic interfaces in the dynamic representation of real-time movement data. I am interested in spatial database development and designing workflows to support the effective use of geographic and spatial information. I have worked in teams digitizing and maintaining GIS data for large-scale utility mapping operations. This experience included working on high definition maps used for autonomous vehicle development and mapping street-lights throughout California.
